# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SnowBuddy Roadshow App is used by investors and startup competition judges during a short, high-pressure roadshow demo. They scan a QR code, enter a name, and immediately experience a simulated ski-team control flow on their own phones. The host may also show the app on a projected desktop browser, starting from Home and switching between Home, Map, and Goggle as needed.

## Product Purpose

SnowBuddy demonstrates the core value of an intelligent ski goggle system: group skiers should not need complex maps, phone calls, group chats, or frequent phone checks to stay connected. The roadshow app is a stable local simulation, not a consumer app or real hardware bridge. Success means judges understand within 30 seconds that the phone is the team control hub and the goggle HUD shows only the most useful cue: who to follow, where to meet, who needs help, and how far away it is.

## Brand Personality

Precise, restrained, alpine. The interface should feel like high-end outdoor sports equipment: compact, confident, readable under bright mountain glare, and focused on action rather than social content.

## Anti-references

Do not make the app feel like a social feed, game UI, generic SaaS dashboard, neon sci-fi concept screen, full mapping product, or hardware control panel that implies real Bluetooth or finished goggle integration. Avoid clutter, decorative motion, excessive cards, gratuitous gradients, and any UI that makes the prototype feel less stable than the story it is meant to tell.

## Design Principles

1. Stability sells the story: every screen must work locally without accounts, GPS, microphone access, backend sync, or hardware.
2. The phone controls; the goggle distills: Home can manage state, but the Goggle view must remain sparse and low-distraction.
3. Priority must be obvious: SOS overrides everything, Meet overrides ordinary tracking, and Voice is brief.
4. Roadshow comprehension beats feature breadth: each interaction must be explainable in five seconds.
5. Product familiarity over spectacle: use standard, trustworthy app controls with a polished alpine equipment tone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The app should target readable mobile-first UI with high contrast, large touch targets, visible focus states, and reduced-motion alternatives. Body text must meet WCAG AA contrast, and SOS cannot rely on red alone; use label, contrast, and layout emphasis together.
